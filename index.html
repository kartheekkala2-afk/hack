The Crisis

The World Has Enough Food. So Why Are Millions Starving?

"Millions of people around the world still struggle to find their next meal — not because food doesn't exist, but because of broken systems."

Food insecurity affects people in every country. While some communities overflow with surplus, others fight daily for survival. The gap between abundance and starvation is not a supply problem — it is a justice problem.

Root Causes

Why Hunger Persists in a World of Plenty

Understanding the interconnected causes of food insecurity is the first step toward building lasting solutions.

💸

Poverty

Over 700 million people live on less than $2.15 per day, making consistent access to nutritious food impossible regardless of supply chains.

38% of cause
🌡️

Climate Change

Extreme weather, droughts, and floods are destroying crops and disrupting farming cycles, disproportionately affecting vulnerable regions.

26% of cause
🗑️

Food Waste

One-third of all food produced globally is wasted — enough to feed every hungry person on Earth two to three times over if redirected.

21% of cause
⚖️

Unequal Distribution

Political conflicts, broken supply chains, and systemic inequality prevent food from reaching those who need it most, even when surplus exists nearby.

15% of cause
